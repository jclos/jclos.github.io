About Me

I did my PhD in the Robert Gordon University, with a SICSA Prize studentship to research argumentation stance mining in social media. I have been lucky to hold many different roles, from software engineering and data science positions in a tech startup in Edinburgh to several research and teaching contracts in the University of Toulouse, the Robert Gordon University, and the University of Nottingham. I have worked in many teams and with a multitude of people of different backgrounds: software engineers, computer scientists, petroleum geologists, education researchers, and many I am forgetting, which has given me a taste for interdisciplinary research. If you share this taste and are looking for a collaboration, feel free to get in touch with me.

Education

PhD Thesis

Robert Gordon University (Aberdeen, Scotland)

Title: Representation and Learning Schemes for Argumentation Mining

Team: Prof. Nirmalie Wiratunga, Dr. Stewart Massie, Prof. Joemon Jose, Dr. Guillaume Cabanac

Abstract: This project focuses on the study of representation and learning schemes for argumentation stance mining from social media content, in order to enable fine-grained debate analysis. Funding for this research was provided by a SICSA prize studentship in the themes of Next Generation Internet and Modelling and Abstraction. I particularly focus on the use of a shallow computational graph for stance lexicon extraction.

Masters Thesis

University of Toulouse (Toulouse, France)

Title: Predicting Entry Points in Q&A Systems

Team: Dr. Guillaume Cabanac, Prof. Mohand Boughanem, Dr. Henri Prade

Abstract: This work is an investigation in engineering features to enhance entry point prediction in Q&A systems. We focus on modelling answer complexity using compression ratios. This research was done in the information retrieval team of the University of Toulouse computer science research laboratory (IRIT).
