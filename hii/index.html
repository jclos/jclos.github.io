Core ideas of the module

Understanding the challenges of human-AI interaction from a sociological and technical perspective.

This module is part of the Human-Computer Interaction theme, and the Artificial Intelligence and Optimisation theme in the School of Computer Science. It is an introduction to the design of human-AI interaction to ensure the AI-driven systems we built are beneficial and useful to people. It covers practical design topics including methods and techniques such as natural language processing and human-robot interaction, but also considers societal and theoretical concerns of human-AI interaction, including the ethics of AI, responsible innovation, trust, accountability and explainable AI. The practical component of the module involves building AI-driven systems that drive conversational experiences, such as a text-based ‘chatbots’ and speech-controlled services, involving automatic speech recognition and natural language processing.
